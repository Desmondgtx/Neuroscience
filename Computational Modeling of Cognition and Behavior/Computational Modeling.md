
### Definition:
Computational modeling is the implementation of a theory with mathematical specification that generates precise, quantitative predictions about behavior and cognition

### Characteristics:
1) **Sufficienty:** The model must be complete enough to actually generate behavior, every component must be defined and specified
2) **Clarity:** Every assumptions are explicit and transparent
3) Falsifiability: Models make precise predictions that can be tested against data
4) **Quantitative predictions:** Models generate numerical or probabilistic predictions about dependet variables (response times, accuracy, etc)
5) **Process specificatoins:** Models specify the mechanisms and processes that produce certain behavior
6) **Parameter estimation:** Models include parameters that can be estimated from data to capture individual diffenreces or experimenal conditions
7) **Testability:** Different models can be compared quantitatively using model selection techniques, as [[Learning Rate]]
