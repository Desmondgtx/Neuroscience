
Refers to theories in psychology and cognitive science that are expressed purely in natural language without formal mathematical specification. This is a significant limitation in the field.

### Characteristics:

1. **Ambiguity**: Verbal theories often contain terms and relationships that can be interpreted in multiple ways, making it difficult to formalize it and derive precise predictions
2. **Lack of precision**: Without mathematical formalization, it's hard to specify exactly how different components of a theory interact
3. **Difficulty in falsification**: Verbal theories can often be stretched or reinterpreted post-hoc to accommodate almost any pattern of data, making them difficult to falsify. **(IMPORTANT)**

This is why the authors propose moving from verbal descriptions like "memory decays over time" to precise mathematical formulas or [[Computational Modeling of Cognition and Behavior/Chapter 1]], like exponential decay functions with specific parameters to asses the decay of the memory.
