### [[Learning Rate]]

**Power Law:** The effect of practice
$$RT = N^{-β}$$
Where:
- RT: Time to perform the task
- N: Number of the trial
- β: Learning Rate Parameter (Value between 0.3 and 0.6)

So, for a β of 0.4 and the 10th trial: 
$$RT=10^{-0.4} = 0.398$$

Heathcore et al. (2000) argued that the data is better described by an alternative exponential funcion given by: 
$$RT=e^{-αN}$$

Where α is the learning rate parameter and e ≈ 2.71
So for α = 0.1 and the 10th trial: 
$$RT = e^{-0.1*10} = e^{-1} = 0.368$$

### Falsifiability

**Falsifiability:** How much a model can handle andhow easy it is to show that it is wrong

"Falsifiability" and "testable" are synonims to denote that, at least in principle, there are some possible outcomes that are incompatible with the theory's predictions

**WE WANT** the theories to be falsifiable, we want to be assured that there are at least *hypothetical*  outcomes that would falsify a theory

Falsifiable =/= False


### Modeling as a "Cognitive Aid" for the Scientist

**Science depends on reproducibility**

Scientist often assumes that we are all reasoning in the same way, and that all scientists have a shared understanding of whatever theory is under condition

To understand and comunicate the notion in a theory, scientist can refer to the concept as they understand it better (e.g., spread activation as electricity or water) but this allows each individual or group to the subjective interpretation of this verbal theory

"Render a computational model more readily communicable and hence more **testeable and falsifiable**"

Computational models can serve as a "cognitive aid for theorists"
